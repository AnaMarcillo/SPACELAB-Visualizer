Step 1: Introduction

You will see the homepage where you will find relevant information. Review it if needed. On the top left, you have different tabs like EXPERIMENTS, where you can view all the databases we are handling in this version, which includes approximately 10 mouse experiments and one human experiment for comparison. Here you will find descriptions, details, and methodologies of these experiments. In Guide, you will see a step-by-step guide on how to use our platform, and in About Us, you can find information about our team. Our focus is the VISUALIZER tab, so click there! ;)

Step 2: Anatomy Overview

Here you will observe a mouse of the species Mus musculus, and you have two buttons below to observe its anatomy.

Step 3: Organs

If you click on ORGANS, you will see all its organs. Here you can scroll and click on different organs to check if there are experiments related to them at this version.

Step 4: Experiment Information

If you select an experiment, you can view its information in the right panel, including a brief description, details, and a visualization option.

Step 5: Visualization

By clicking on Visualize, you will be taken to the most important part of our platform, where you will see MultiQC reports, which are reports of all the data generated by the experiments in HTML format. Here you can visualize and interactively explore the data according to the expected information. Within this same menu, you can visualize various experiments and different types of reports; use your knowledge and expertise to navigate through them.

Step 6: Returning

If you click on the return menu, you can see the anatomical diagram of the mouse again and explore its muscles. Different details of the model will also be presented. You can repeat the action.

Step 7: AI Integration

This platform will include a lightweight GPT-2 model trained with data from bioinformatics, mouse experiments, methodologies, and information from various datasets in the NASA open repository, where users can ask questions and access information to better understand the data.
